Fisheries secy reviews research on drones in Kolkata


The review is significant as the Fisheries Department has assigned a pilot project on “Developing drone technology for live fish transport” to ICAR-CIFRI, aiming to design and develop a 100-kg payload drone carrying live fish up to 10 km, said a statement by the Ministry of Fisheries.




Fisheries Secretary Dr Abhilaksh Likhi visited the Kolkata-based ICAR- Central lnland Fisheries Research Institute (CIFRI) Tuesday and reviewed the research and development activities of the institute regarding drone application for fisheries management.

The review is significant as the Fisheries Department has assigned a pilot project on “Developing drone technology for live fish transport” to ICAR-CIFRI, aiming to design and develop a 100-kg payload drone carrying live fish up to 10 km, said a statement by the Ministry of Fisheries.

“Different drone-based technologies viz. sprayer drone, feed broadcast drone and cargo delivery drone were demonstrated by ICAR-CIFRI and start-up companies among more than 100 fishermen and fisherwomen. The pilot project undertaken by ICAR-CIFRI will open a new horizon in the fisheries section by providing an effective and promising alternative for transporting fresh fish with less time and minimum human involvement while minimising stress to the fish,” it said.

According to the statement, Dr Likhi urged the ICAR-CIFRI and other stakeholders to take steps for making these drone-based applications reach fish farmers. He also asked the fisheries department to document the demonstrations and send them to the ministry so that they can be utilised to create awareness among fish farmers.

———-

ICAR-CIFRI Director Dr B K Das presented the institute’s achievements and progress made in drone-based technologies, it said.

The research and development on fish transportation using drone technology with private partnership would also enable consumers and farmers to have better hygienic fresh fish in the supply chain system, it said.
